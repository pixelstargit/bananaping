  • What we believe

We are a loose collection of academics, designers, journalists, artists, filmmakers, architects and designers and urban culture practitioners who share an interest in exploring alternative perspectives on urban life in Africa, Latin America and Asia without the filter of the west. Collectively and individually we create exhibitions, produce books, films and events that present perspectives of the city in the most populous parts of the world. We love good ideas and see ourselves as advocates for grounded action. We seek to facilitate an understanding of context towards building equitable and just cities across the global south and the rest of the world. 

Our flagship project is the bi-annual publication Cityscapes Magazine which is published in collaboration with African Centre for Cities (South Africa); Indian Institute for Human Settlements (India) and Urbam (Colombia)

Core Team

Tau Tavengwa (Co-Founder).
Tau is founder of Cityscapes Magazine which he also edits. A designer, his work sits at the nexus of creativity, academic research and urban practice. It outputs in various media including publication, film and exhibitions

EDGAR PIETERSE (Co-Founder)
Edgar is Director of African Centre for Cities at University of Cape Town. He holds the South African Research Chair in Urban Policy. His work looks at African cities as part of larger discourses on sustainable urban transitions and southern urbanism.

SEAN O'TOOLE(Co-Editor)
Sean O’Toole is an author, critic, journalist and editor based in Cape Town. His essays, cultural journalism and reviews have appeared in numerous books, newspapers and magazines, including Artforum, Frieze, Harvard Design Magazine and Tate Etc.
